What is period poverty?

It refers to the lack of access to sanitation facilities, menstrual products because of financial burden due to the high cost of sanitation and hygiene.

Here are some of the initiatives taken by the girl up organisation

  • In many countries, menstrual products are still listed as a luxury product, meaning you may get taxed for buying them. encouraging our government to legally recognise it as an essential product, as no one can control their period.
  • Periods, in general, are highly stigmatised. This is one of the reasons period poverty rates are so high. By simply having conversations about periods with your friends, you can help de-stigmatise the issue and become more comfortable talking about them. You will probably realise that everyone with a period has had their embarrassing period moments, and you will feel less alone.
  • There are many charities that are working hard to put an end to period poverty. Some charities ask for money, while others ask for donations of menstrual products. ActionAid is a charity dedicated to helping people in poverty around the world. In Kind Direct is a charity which donates unused items, including sanitary products, to those who cannot afford such things.
